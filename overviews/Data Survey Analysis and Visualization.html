Overview

This Power BI project analyzes a survey dataset to uncover key insights into the data profession. The goal is to understand salary distribution, job satisfaction, programming languages, and demographic factors that influence career paths in data-related roles. This interactive dashboard is designed to help both professionals and employers better understand the current trends in the industry and identify key areas of improvement.

Dataset

This project uses survey data collected from professionals in data-related roles, featuring demographic information and responses to various questions about job satisfaction, career paths, and programming preferences. The dataset includes the following information:

  • Unique ID: A unique identifier for each respondent.
  • Email: Contact email of the respondent.
  • Country, City, and Role: Geographic location and professional title.
  • Salary & Job Satisfaction: Data on salary figures and responses regarding job satisfaction in areas like salary, work-life balance, and management.
  • Programming Language Preferences: Favorite programming languages used by survey participants.
  • Demographics: Information on age, gender, education, and ethnicity.

Key Insights Delivered

  • Salary Trends by Job Title: The stacked bar chart shows salary distribution across various data roles, providing insights into pay gaps between junior and senior-level positions.
  • Regional Salary Distribution: The treemap visualizes how salary varies across different countries, helping identify high-paying regions for data professionals.
  • Programming Language Popularity: A stacked column chart reveals the most commonly used programming languages among data professionals, offering insights into industry trends and skills demand.
  • Job Satisfaction Analysis: The gauge visuals track survey responses to satisfaction metrics such as salary, work-life balance, and career progression, highlighting areas where employers can improve.
  • Gender Salary Disparity: The donut chart explores the average salary differences between male and female respondents, identifying potential gaps in compensation across genders.

Methodology

  • Data was cleaned and transformed using Power Query Editor in Power BI to ensure consistency and remove anomalies.
  • Key metrics and aggregations, such as average salary, happiness scores, and gender-based salary distribution, were calculated using DAX expressions.
  • Interactive visualizations were created to allow for deeper exploration of the data, with slicers and tooltips providing enhanced interactivity.
  • The dashboard was designed for ease of use, ensuring that stakeholders could filter data by job title, age, and region to uncover targeted insights.

Outcome

This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of the state of the data profession, offering actionable insights into salary trends, job satisfaction, and career paths.
